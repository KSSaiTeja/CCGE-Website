What is EA (Enrolled Agent)?

The Highest U.S. Tax Credential You Can Earn

EA is a prestigious U.S. tax certification authorized by the IRS (Internal Revenue Service). It requires you to clear 3 exams: Individuals, Businesses, and Representation. The international administration process for the India region is now permanently available, making it accessible to Indian professionals.

  • IRS Authorized Certification - The highest U.S. tax credential
  • No Prerequisites Required - Ideal for graduates, CAs, or anyone interested in U.S. taxation
  • Permanent Availability in India - International administration now available
  • Three Comprehensive Exams - Individuals, Businesses, and Representation
  • Global Recognition - Practice before IRS nationwide

EA Exam Structure

The EA certification consists of three comprehensive exams that cover all aspects of U.S. taxation and representation before the IRS.

Part 1: Individuals

Comprehensive coverage of individual tax preparation, including:

Key Topics:
  • Individual income tax filing requirements
  • Income and deductions for individuals
  • Tax credits and payments
  • Special situations and taxpayer compliance

Part 2: Businesses

Business taxation covering various entity types and their tax implications:

  • Business Income and Expenses
  • Depreciation and Amortization
  • Partnership Taxation
  • Corporate Taxation
  • S-Corporation Taxation
  • Estate and Gift Tax

Part 3: Representation, Practices & Procedures

Professional standards and representation before the IRS:

Focus Areas:
  • IRS examination and collection procedures
  • Professional ethics and responsibilities
  • Taxpayer rights and appeals process
  • Penalties and interest calculations

Why Choose EA Certification?

Perfect for Indian Professionals: EA certification is ideal for graduates, CAs, or anyone interested in U.S. taxation. With no specific educational qualifications required, it opens doors to lucrative opportunities in international tax consulting and U.S. tax preparation services.
